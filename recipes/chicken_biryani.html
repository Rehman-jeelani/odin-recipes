Chicken Biryani

Description

Chicken Biryani is a fragrant and flavorful South Asian rice dish made with marinated chicken, basmati rice, and a blend of aromatic spices. Layered with fried onions, saffron, and herbs, this iconic dish is slow-cooked to perfection, allowing the flavors to infuse deeply. Known for its rich taste and royal appeal, chicken biryani is a celebration of tradition, perfect for special occasions or hearty everyday meals.

Ingredients

For Chicken Marinate:

  • 1 kg chicken (bone-in, cut into medium pieces)
  • 1 cup plain yogurt (dahi)
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • Handful of chopped fresh mint & coriander leaves

For Rice:

  • 2 cups basmati rice (soaked 30 mins)
  • 6 cups water
  • 2–3 cloves
  • 2–3 green cardamoms
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 small cinnamon stick
  • Salt (to taste)

For Biryani Assembly:

  • 2 large onions (thinly sliced and fried until golden brown)
  • 2 tbsp ghee or butter
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • A few saffron strands soaked in ¼ cup warm milk (optional)
  • Extra mint & coriander for layering
  • 1 tsp kewra or rose water (optional, for aroma)

Steps

  1. Marinate the Chicken:In a large bowl, combine chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, coriander powder, salt, lemon juice, mint, and coriander leaves. Mix well, cover, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour (or overnight for best flavor).
  2. Prepare the Rice:Wash and soak basmati rice for 30 minutes. In a large pot, boil 6 cups of water with whole spices (cloves, cardamom, bay leaf, cinnamon) and salt. Add soaked rice and parboil (70% cooked). Drain and set aside.
  3. Fry Onions:In a large pan, heat oil and fry the sliced onions until golden brown. Remove half for layering later.
  4. The Marinated Chicken:In the same pan, add the marinated chicken and cook on medium heat until chicken is tender and masala thickens. Do not overcook. If needed, add a splash of water.
  5. Layer the Biryani:In a deep pot, layer half the cooked chicken, then half the rice. Sprinkle some fried onions, mint, coriander, saffron milk (optional), and a little ghee. Repeat with remaining chicken and rice. Finish with garnishes.
  6. Dum (Steam Cook):Cover the pot with a tight lid (or seal with dough) and cook on very low heat for 20–25 minutes.
  7. Serve:Gently fluff the biryani and serve hot with raita or salad.
