Gol Gappay

Description

Gol Gappay, also known as Pani Puri or Puchka, are crispy hollow balls filled with a spicy blend of tangy tamarind water, mashed potatoes, chickpeas, and flavorful chutneys.

With just one bite, you get a perfect explosion of sweet, sour, spicy, and crunchy — all at once!A true street food favorite, Gol Gappay are tiny delights packed with big flavor. Once you start, you can’t stop!

Ingredients

For Puris (hollow shells):

  • Semolina (sooji/rava)
  • Maida (plain flour), water, a pinch of salt
  • Oil for kneading & deep frying

For Spiced water (“pani”):

  • Fresh mint leaves
  • Fresh coriander (cilantro)
  • Green chilies
  • Ginger
  • Tamarind or lime/lemon juice
  • Spices: roasted cumin, chaat masala, black salt, asafoetida
  • Cold water (often 3–4 cups)

For Stuffing:

  • Boiled & mashed potatoes
  • Boiled chickpeas (white or kala chana)
  • Finely chopped onion (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro
  • Spices: cumin powder, chaat masala, chili powder, black salt, pepper

For Chutneys & Toppings:

  • Mint‑coriander chutney
  • Tamarind-date (sweet) chutney
  • Sweet yogurt (optional, for dahi puri variation): yogurt + sugar
  • Garnishes: red chili powder, sev, fresh cilantro

Steps

  1. Combine semolina, all-purpose flour, and salt in a bowl.
  2. Knead the mixture into a firm, smooth dough using water.
  3. Cover the dough and let it rest for 30 to 45 minutes.
  4. Roll out the dough thinly and cut into small, round discs.
  5. Deep-fry the discs in medium-hot oil until they puff up and turn golden brown.
  6. Transfer the puris to a wire rack or paper towel and allow them to cool completely.
  7. Blend mint leaves, coriander, green chili, ginger, and tamarind or lemon juice with a small amount of water.
  8. Strain the blended mixture and add additional cold water to dilute.
  9. Stir in roasted cumin powder, chaat masala, and black salt to taste.
  10. Refrigerate the spicy water until ready to serve.
  11. In a separate bowl, combine tamarind pulp with jaggery or sugar, spices, and water to prepare the sweet water.
  12. Mix well until fully dissolved and chill before serving.
  13. Mash boiled potatoes and mix them with boiled chickpeas.
  14. Season the filling with cumin powder, chaat masala, red chili powder, salt, and chopped coriander.
  15. Gently crack the top of each puri to create an opening.
  16. Fill each puri with the prepared stuffing mixture.
  17. Optionally add a spoonful of chutney or yogurt for extra flavor.
  18. Dip the filled puris into the chilled spicy or sweet water.
  19. Serve immediately and enjoy in one bite for the best taste and texture.
